About Tuni – The Mango City

Tuni is a beautiful town located in the Kakinada district of Andhra Pradesh, India. It is famously known as the "Mango City" due to its vast mango orchards and exports of premium-quality mangoes. Tuni lies between Visakhapatnam and Rajahmundry, making it a well-connected town with both cultural richness and natural charm.

History and Significance

The town of Tuni holds a rich historical legacy, dating back to the colonial era and beyond. It has been an important trade center in the East Godavari region and has seen rapid development over the years. The local traditions, temples, and fairs reflect the deep-rooted cultural values of Andhra Pradesh.

Cultural Heritage

Tuni is known for its vibrant festivals like Sankranti, Ugadi, and Diwali, which are celebrated with great enthusiasm. Traditional dances, classical music, and religious rituals are an integral part of the local lifestyle. The people here are friendly, welcoming, and take pride in preserving their cultural roots.

Economy and Agriculture

Tuni is primarily an agricultural town, with mango cultivation being the major economic activity. It also produces cashew, coconut, and rice in abundance. The town has local markets and businesses that support trade in food processing, textiles, and retail sectors.
